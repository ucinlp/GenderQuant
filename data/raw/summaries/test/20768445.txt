Upon the apparent death of his taxi-driver father , Xian-chuen  and his mother Li  take in his father's last fare, Su-Dan , a bar girl, to fulfill the family's obligations. The father, Wu Tao drove his cab into the Yangtze River, breaking Su-Dan's leg and potentially paralyzing her for life, though his body was not recovered. The son, a bereaved and sullen teenager, does not take to the crippled Su-Dan at first, his thoughts preoccupied with a school crush and the sudden death of his father. As time goes on, however he warms to her presence . Su-Dan, restricted to a wheelchair sees the boy as a substitute little brother. Meanwhile, Li, who works as a medic at a factory, is forced to work double shifts at a veterinarian hospital to help make ends meet. There she meets a handsome stranger  who brings in his injured dog, and a tentative romance begins to form.
