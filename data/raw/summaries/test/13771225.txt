A married couple struggles to adjust when the husband's brain is transplanted into the skull of a black man. David Rowe  is a white district attorney who must now live his life as a black man. His wife Margaret  tries to deal with the transformation of her husband's appearance as David feels the stings of racial prejudice for the first time. Sheriff Webb  is a local lawman who resents the district attorney, but after the sheriff kills his own black mistress, he must rely on David for his legal defense. Margaret has trouble being intimate with the man she knows is still her husband. Rowe investigates the murder of the young black woman while his superiors, friends and family treat him differently.
