State College football coach George Cooper  has more than enough problems on the job without his teenage daughter Connie  complicating his life at home. She is convinced she's unattractive to the opposite sex and wallows in self pity. Resigning herself to a loveless existence, she decides to make literature her life. When a fictional article she pens about a teenage bubble dancer appears in a confessions magazine, the boys come calling. The young author dates a high school football star from across town who chooses to attend State College  to be near his new found sweetheart. George's gridiron and domestic problems are solved.
