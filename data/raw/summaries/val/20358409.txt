David Kolowitz  works as a delivery boy and assistant for a machine shop in New York City in 1938, and is fascinated with the movies. Despite the misgivings of his girlfriend Wanda , his parents  and his employer , David follows the suggestion of a friend  and becomes involved with an off-Broadway theater company run by Harrison B. Marlowe . He admires Ronald Colman so he uses the stage name "Donald Colman." It is a margin operation that requires him to pay $5 a week for "tuition." Marlowe's daughter  takes a romantic interest in David, who perseveres despite a lack of acting talent and the hostility of Marlowe. Overcoming all the difficulties, he makes his acting debut and his parents and girlfriend accept his new interest.
