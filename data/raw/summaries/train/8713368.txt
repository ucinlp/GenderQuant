The movie revolves around A.C. Gilbert, his family and toy factory. The film is set during the First World War. Gilbert, a successful toymaker, is requested by the government to re-tool his factory to help produce goods for the war effort. Gilbert initially agrees to this, but comes to regret his decision. The movie also deals with Gilbert's relationship with his father  and his son . Things get the better of Gilbert as he learns that his brother  has been declared missing in action in the war. This and other factors cause Gilbert to confront the government over plans to encourage people not to celebrate Christmas in order to save resources for the war effort. Gilbert successfully lobbies the government to allow him  to resume the production of toys for Christmas. Thus earning him the name The man who saved Christmas. In the final scene Gilbert's brother returns from the war in time to celebrate Christmas. The film contains numerous errors and omissions: Al Junior is portrayed as an only child yet, in real life, he had two older sisters, Charlotte and Lucretia.
