The Frake family heads off for the Iowa State Fair - each with their own hopes for the trip. Mother Melissa  hopes her pickles and mincemeat will beat a rival's. Father Abel  is bringing his prize boar, Blue Boy, to show. Their son Wayne  has a lesson to teach to one of the game barkers, and is upset when his girl can't go to the fair this year. And Margy  just wants something to pull her out of her melancholy mood, as portrayed in the song, "It Might as Well Be Spring". All members of the family have their own adventures in store during their days at the fair, returning to the family camper each evening with their own thoughts and secrets. Margy spends her days and evenings with newspaper writer Pat ... will he be the change she's looking for? Wayne woos singer Emily ; how will that affect his relationship with the girl back home?
