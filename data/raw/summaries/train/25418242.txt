Kunjumon  lands up in Kochi with an infant in hand. He approaches a teashop owner played by Innocent, with a recommendation letter, who says there is no scope for him there as the shop is on the verge of closing down. By the time the credits end, the business has turned around and the infant has grown up. The boy grows up to be a strapping young man Alby , dancing on the beaches of Goa with his father and a bunch of dancers for company. We see that the father too has 'grown up,' sporting a handle bar moustache with salt and pepper hair. He is now the owner of a successful catering firm, managed by his former employer. Everything is hunky dory with some inane humour passed on in the name of entertainment. The impending conflict comes in the form of a vow taken by Alby that he will not marry a girl, who laughs at his father.
