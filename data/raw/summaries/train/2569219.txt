Rusty , a chorus girl working at a nightclub run by her boyfriend Danny McGuire , is given a chance for stardom by the wealthy magazine editor John Coudair , who years earlier had been in love with her grandmother, Maribelle Hicks. Offered an opportunity to be a highly-paid cover girl, Rusty would faithfully remain with her nightclub act if only Danny would ask her. He doesn't want to stand in her way, so he picks an argument to send her packing. Rusty becomes a star on Broadway after appearing in a musical produced by Coudair's wealthy friend, Noel Wheaton , and decides to get married to Wheaton. At the last second she leaves the wedding and reunites with Danny.New York Times review summary
