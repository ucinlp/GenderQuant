After the Battle of Worcester at the end of the English Civil War, the main aim of General Oliver Cromwell  is to capture Charles Stuart , son of the executed Charles I. However, the dashing Royalist hero nicknamed "the Moonraker" ([[George Baker  prepares to smuggle him to safety in France, under the noses of Cromwell's soldiers. According to the story, he is called "the Moonraker" after a smuggler term; items would be hidden in the village pond then raked out by moonlight.
