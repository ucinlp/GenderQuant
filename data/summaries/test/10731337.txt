In a small village in Romania, a local police constable frames Johann Moritz  as being Jewish, because his wife Suzanna has refused his advances. He is sent to a concentration camp as punishment. Eventually he is deemed part of the Aryan race and obliged to enlist as a Waffen SS soldier. After the war, he is arrested and prosecuted as a war criminal, but is ultimately released and re-united with his wife and sons in Germany. The picture is based on real events. It includes Hungary's government in collaboration with the Nazis, the encroachment of Romania by Joseph Stalin's troopers, and other happenings.
