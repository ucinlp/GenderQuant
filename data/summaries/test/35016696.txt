The film is the story of Alassane’s mother, who has been waiting for her husband for 20 years. It is also the story of his sister, who also waits for her husband who left five years ago, and his niece, who does not know her father. After a two-year absence, Alassane Diago returns to Agnam Lidoubé, a Senegalese village in the Fouta region, to try and understand why and how his mother has spent all these years waiting.<ref nameCissokho|first‘’Les Larmes de l’émigration’’ en compétition officielle au Festival du film francophone de Namur|url11 March 2012|dateAgence de Presse Sénégalaise|locationFrench}} His camera technique captures the inner beauty and strength of the characters.{{cite web}}
