During the turn of the 20th Century, the poor Mancuso family , from Sicily, Italy, emigrates to the United States. They dream of the land of opportunity, where giant vegetables are grown, people swim in milk, and coins fall from the sky. Salvatore takes his family, as well as his old mother, Fortunata . While on the ship to America, many men find the British Lucy  attractive. For administrative reasons, Lucy wants to marry Salvatore on arrival on Ellis Island. He agrees; he understands that she is not in love with him yet, but expects that will come. At Ellis Island, the families undergo extensive and humiliating physical and psychological examinations and questioning. Pietro  is about to be sent back for being mute, and Salvatore's mother may be sent back for insufficient intelligence.
