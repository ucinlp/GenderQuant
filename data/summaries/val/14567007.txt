A professor of astronomy  gives a lecture instructing on an impending solar eclipse. The class rushes to an observation tower to witness the event, which features an anthropomorphic Sun and Moon coming together. The "effeminate" Sun lick their lips in anticipation as the eclipse arrives, culminating in a "homosexual" encounter between the two celestial bodies.Carolyn Jess-Cooke  Film sequels: theory and practice from Hollywood to Bollywood, Edinburgh University Press, p20 Various heavenly bodies, including planets and moons, hang in the night sky; a meteor shower is depicted using the ghostly figures of girls. The professor of astronomy, shocked by all he has witnessed, topples from the observation tower.
