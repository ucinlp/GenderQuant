The film is a fictionalized biography of Fred Fisher, a German-born American writer of Tin Pan Alley songs. Tin Pan Alley promoter ([[Mark Stevens  turns serious composer Fred Breitenbach  into songwriter Fred Fisher. Fred Fisher is his assumed name in real life and Breitenbach is his birth surname. In the film, many Fisher songs were given a symphonic arrangement that was performed at Aeolian Hall. Among the Fisher songs heard were: * Chicago * Dardanella * Peg O' My Heart * Who paid the rent for Mrs. Rip Van Winkle?   {{OCLC}} {{OCLC}}
