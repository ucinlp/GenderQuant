Kitty  is found working on the streets picking pockets by the painter Thomas Gainsborough  and agrees to sit for a portrait for him. She then attracts the attention of wealthy playboy Sir Hugh Marcy  who, upon finding out her real social status, uses her for revenge against a former colleague to whom he owes the loss of his job in the foreign office by having Kitty pose as a lady of fashion. What Sir Hugh does not count on is the attraction Kitty develops for him.
