The film is by Eric Ellena and Berna Huebner,{{cite web}} and is narrated by actress Olivia de Havilland.{{cite web}} It features an interview with Yasmin Aga Khan, president of Alzheimer Disease International and daughter of Rita Hayworth, who had Alzheimer's, describing how her mother took up painting while struggling with the disease.{{cite web}} The documentary includes interviews with renowned neurologists who explain how creative activities engage areas of the brain that are not damaged by the disease and thus reawaken a sense of personality, identity and dignity. Doctors interviewed include Robert Neil Butler, founding director of the National Institute of Aging;{{cite web}} The film demonstrates the intersection between the arts, medical and scientific worlds.{{cite web}}
