France, late 1920. Thérèse married her neighbor Bernard Desqueyroux, charming and free spirited. They then join their property located in a vast area. Bernard has a strong character, but she is still fascinated by this young man and his opinions. However, she is quickly stifled by the tedium of provincial life and the intellectual mediocrity of her husband. She dreams of Paris, aims to stimulate and culture, and despite herself, starts looking for a way to get by. Until the day Bernard gets drunk mortally with arsenic. Teresa is discovered and, in addition to being dishonored by his own family, it is also that of her deceased husband.
