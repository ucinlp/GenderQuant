Young d'Artagnan leaves his parents and travels from his native Gascogne to the capitol of France because he wants to prove himself an excellent fencer and to become a musketeer. He is told by his father he must not avoid any duel. On his way to Paris d'Artagnan feels that his honour is besmirched because he overhears how his horse is derided by a sinister nobleman. He can't help but demand immediate satisfaction. Unfortunately of all things he has hereby challenged Count de Rochefort, a shifty character to whom Cardinal Richelieu frequently entrusts covert operations. Count de Rochefort's henchmen take care of d'Artagnan and steal from him. The enraged d'Artagnan is determined to take revenge. He will eventually have the chance for the Queen his given a present to the Duke of Buckingham and d'Artagnan must retrieve it from her secret admirer who is already back in England. If he fails her, Cardinal Richelieu is going disclose the Queen Anne's infidelity to King Louis XIII in order to force a war against England upon him. The Cardinal and Count de Rochefort will do everything in their power if only they can put paid to d'Artagnan's mission. But with help from his three new friends d'Artagnan prevails.
