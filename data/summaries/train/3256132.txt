Neil Klugman  is a highly intelligent, working class army veteran and graduate of Rutgers University who earns a living as a library clerk. He falls in love with Brenda Patimkin , a wealthy student at Radcliffe College who is at home for the summer. They face obstacles from Brenda's family , due to differences in class and assimilation into the American mainstream. More imposing conflicts include morality and propriety differences related to the premarital sex and the possibilities of ill-timed pregnancies.
