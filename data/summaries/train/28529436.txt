Monique Mélinand portrays a woman in the late stages of terminal illness. Her son Philippe , Philippe's wife Nathalie , and her husband Roger  attempt to comfort her as she navigates through her ordeal. However, those two closest men in her personal life begin to get more involved in their relationships with multiple mistresses. Her husband flirts with customers in their clothing and haberdashery store while her son flirts with her nurses. The film incorporates elements of Mozart’s opera Così fan tutte to poetic effect, relating to these scenes.{{citeweb}}
